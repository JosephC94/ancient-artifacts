When Arthur died of his wounds following his battle with Mordred, it should have been returned to its source, the Lady of the Lake, rather than being entrusted to whichever knight - no matter how noble - might succeed Arthur as king. This rule did not hold true as Arthur presented the sword to a knight named Sir Gawain, who then loaned it to Sir Lancelot for his defense of Guinevere. Gawain then returned the sword to King Arthur for his final battle with Mordred and, afterwards, returned it to The Lady of The Lake.{% endblock %} {% block p3 %}The sword posses power as during a battle between the Saxons and Britons, The Saxons held the high ground and inflicted heavy casualties on the Britons. The Saxons continued to hold their position until the day was almost gone before Arthur had enough, and lead a final charge on their position himself. Drawing Excalibur he called upon God and slew his enemies, one by one, with a single blow to each. He did not once slacken in his onslaught until he had slain four hundred and seventy men single-handedly. This, when the Britons beheld, they followed him up in close rank dealing slaughter on every side.{% endblock %} {% block p4 %}The sword was discovered at the bottom of a frozen lake in the heart of the Forest of Dean.
